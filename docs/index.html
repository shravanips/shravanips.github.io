Hi, I’m Shravani 👋

— though you can also call me Shravu or Aani.

I’m an MS Computer Science (Cybersecurity) student at Stevens Institute of Technology, Hoboken, NJ.

My work lives at the intersection of AI security, cybersecurity, and reliable machine learning infrastructure. I enjoy taking technical ideas from research or coursework and shaping them into practical systems, experiments, and tools that can actually be used, tested, and improved.

Currently, I work on research advised by Dr. Hao Wang at the IntelliSys Lab, where I study how reinforcement learning systems behave under partial failures and design experiments to evaluate system stability and performance degradation.

My research interests include AI Security, Reinforcement Learning, AI Systems, Machine Learning, High Performance Computing, and Cybersecurity with Data Analysis. I’m especially drawn to problems where systems, security, and intelligent behavior come together.

Outside of research, I love capturing moments using my Fujifilm camera. I usually walk around my neighborhood in Hoboken on Sundays, or head to nearby places to click street photographs.
You can explore some of that work in the Media section or visit my photography page 📸 @shravu.frames.

I also enjoy travelling, trekking, writing, and reading books — small things that keep me curious, grounded, and creatively inspired.
